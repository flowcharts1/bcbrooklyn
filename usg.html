Robert Echevarria: For the last 3 years (since the 1st Semester of my Freshman Year), I have dedicated myself to answering the needs of the thousands of students at BC through USG. In 23–24, I shepherded the Neuroscience Major (that we have now) through the Faculty Council’s Committee on Undergraduate Curriculum and Degree Requirements. In 24–25, I wrote legislation that installed a hook in every bathroom stall on campus so people can hang their belongings, and I helped pass and made edits to USG’s ‘Undocumented Student Protection Resolution’ that started discussions that turned into policies we have that protects Undocumented and other Vulnerable Students on campus. In 25–26, I wrote legislation that mandates the Library and W.E.B Building to constantly replace the markers that constantly dry out in the Study Rooms—since I believe no CUNY student should be paying out of pocket for anything which tuition dollars are supposed to cover. As the senior-most member of the USG Senate (and longest serving Member of USG aside from our Current President), I ask again for your signature and vote—not to be your Senator for a 4th year, but to be Your President.

Illan Saji: As a fellow student and peer mentor, I believe that our maximum potential is achieved through the strong community and opportunities found at BC. I have firsthand experience of this through my three semesters as Senator and even more within USG. This includes collaborating with student body affairs, making campus resources more accessible to all, and assisting with allocation of tech fees (included in our tuition) towards campus upgrades. Thus, I want to continue advocating for necessities that make us stronger as a whole. Outside USG, I am involved as AMSA Vice President and within Kappa Sigma, Newman Center, and more. I love attending campus events and will continue to support all BC student-led clubs and organizations as your USG Vice President. I am also Peer Coach for Macaulay Honors, supporting students through their academic careers while connecting them with on-campus resources. Using my established leadership, network, and passion for our community, I will push for favorable policies that encourage student success and serve our diverse community, using the platform of USG Executive Cabinet to enhance our campus for years to come.
